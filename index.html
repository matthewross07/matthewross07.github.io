Theme

Building cities, farming crops, and mining ore all have changed the face of the earth. In many of these altered places, human design decisions control environmental processes more than the processes and co-evolution in natural systems. I study these designed landscapes asking:

1) How do these designed ecosystems work?

2) What feedbacks, ecological, legal, or social, are most important in structuring these places?

3) Can we use our knowledge from 1 and 2 to design ecosystems better?

Research

My research covers a broad ragne of topics and you can see more on my Google Scholar. Many ecosystems are heavily impacted by humans, but not many are as heavily controlled by people as the mountaintop mined landscapes of Central Appalachia. Despite the deep and widespread impacts of mining for coal from the surface, we don't understand basic questions about mining and its impact on the environment. For my PhD research, I am looking at:

1) How much earth has mining moved & what do these new landscapes look like?
2) How does water and elements move through such heavily altered landscapes?
3) How does understanding questions 1 & 2 help us understand the impacts to downstream water quality from mining? How long will these impacts last?
